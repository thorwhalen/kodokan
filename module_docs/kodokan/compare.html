kodokan.compare

Compare two demonstrations of a throw (training-free — Stage 1).

We represent each frame by a vector of joint angles (e.g. elbow, shoulder, hip, knee flexion). Angles are inherently invariant to translation and scale — and far more occlusion-tolerant than raw pixel coordinates — so no skeleton normalization is required. Two demonstrations are then aligned with Dynamic Time Warping, which absorbs differences in execution speed and yields a similarity distance plus the warping path (reused to draw per-angle deviation).

Caveat (honest): joint angles computed from 2D keypoints are not invariant to camera viewpoint, so two demos shot from very different angles will read as more different than they are. Viewpoint-invariant comparison (JEANIE-style joint temporal+viewpoint warping, or 3D lifting) is the planned Stage-3 upgrade; see misc/docs/research-architecture.md §7.
